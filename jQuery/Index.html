Main takeaways from Stephen King’s book on writing

If you haven’t read Stephen King’s book on writing, here are the most useful tips i found after completion:

• Write your first draft “with the door closed”

That means don’t show it to friends, family, tumblr, etc. until it’s finished to its completion. the second draft is when you can bring it into the light. just get the words down on paper and don’t worry about editing it until it’s all out of your system.

• The road to hell is paved with adverbs

Everyone loves using adverbs in their work, but it’s often the sign of lazy writing or the inability to express what you’re trying to say. for the first draft, it doesn’t matter; on the second draft, try to eliminate as many as you can and rewrite the sentence to work without it.
